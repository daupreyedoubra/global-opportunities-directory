# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Founders, students, and early-career professionals — global, desktop-first, with a
strong African contingent — who open this link from X/WhatsApp between tasks. They are
hunting for grants, accelerators, fellowships, and internships they can still apply to.
Their context: hurried, skeptical, decision-oriented. The job to be done: "tell me in
seconds what's still open, what I qualify for, and when it closes — so I don't waste
2 hours discovering I was never eligible."

## Product Purpose

A verified, deadline-first directory of the 142 opportunities in the widely shared
"2026/2027 Global and Regional Opportunities Directory" PDF (compiled by Abdulsamad
@richtosho). The PDF is a flat table with no deadlines and no eligibility detail; as of
July 2026, 58% of its entries are already closed. This product adds what the PDF lacks:
verified deadlines, concrete eligibility criteria, live open/closed status, and honest
"unverified" flags. Success = a founder finds every still-open opportunity they qualify
for in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Fast, honest, editorial. Three words: **urgent · trustworthy · sharp**. The feel of a
newsroom deadline desk: red pen on white paper, calm authority, zero fluff. Speed-tool
interaction (Linear-grade filtering) with an editorial masthead voice and warm,
encouraging microcopy — never bureaucratic, never hype.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages (gradient hero, identical card grids, eyebrow labels).
- "Opportunities blog" aggregators: ad-choked, SEO-padded, burying the deadline below
  600 words of filler.
- Government portal density-without-hierarchy.
- Cream/beige "AI default" surfaces; AI-purple accents.

## Design Principles

1. **Deadline is the hierarchy.** Days-left is the first thing the eye lands on in
   every row; the list is sorted by it. Everything else supports that scan.
2. **Honesty over completeness.** Closed is labeled closed; unverified is labeled
   unverified with a caution note. Never imply certainty the data doesn't have.
3. **Filter, don't paginate.** One page, instant filtering, no reloads, no dead ends —
   an empty result teaches the user how to widen the net.
4. **Familiarity earns trust.** Standard affordances (search field, select, chips,
   table-like rows). The tool disappears into the task.
5. **Credit is part of the product.** The compiler (@richtosho) is credited visibly;
   this build extends his work, it doesn't appropriate it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text ≥4.5:1, status conveyed by text + color (never color
alone), full keyboard paths, visible focus, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected,
semantic landmarks and labels. Works on mobile (many users arrive from X on phones)
even though desktop is the primary design target.
